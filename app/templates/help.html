Adding a receipt

  1. Go to Receipts → New receipt.
    2. {% if ocr_available %}
  2. Under Scan a receipt, tap to take a photo (on a phone it opens the camera) or choose an image, then hit Scan & auto-fill.
  3. Dilbyrt reads the image and fills in the vendor, date, location, totals, and line items. Review everything and fix anything it misread.
    4. {% else %}
  4. OCR isn't enabled on this server, so attach the image and type the fields in yourself.
    5. {% endif %}
  5. Set how the cost is split (see below), then Save receipt.

Splitting a receipt between businesses

First add your businesses under Businesses. Then on any receipt pick one of:

  • One business — the whole total goes to a single business.
  • Split evenly — pick two (or more) businesses and the total is divided equally (this is your 50/50).
  • Itemized — bill each line item to its own business using the Bill to column.

The dashboard and each receipt show the resulting per-business totals.

Getting your data into Google Sheets

On the Receipts page use Export:

  • CSV or Excel — download the file, then in Google Drive choose New → File upload and open it with Google Sheets. Each business allocation is its own row so per-business totals add up.
  • Push to Google Sheet — appears when an admin has connected a Google service-account key in Settings. It writes straight into your sheet so you can edit on a Chromebook. See the README for the one-time setup.

Roles

  • Viewer — browse receipts and run exports.
  • Editor — everything a viewer can do, plus add/edit/delete receipts and businesses.
  • Admin — everything, plus manage users and settings.
